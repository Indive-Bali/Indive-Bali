SOUTH BALI TOUR

Bali Island is famous for its beautiful beaches, especially in the south. Most tourists visiting Bali opt for destinations in the southern region to make the most of their time. Bali’s South is home to some of the island’s most iconic beaches, temples, and cultural sites.

Uluwatu Temple

Uluwatu Temple is one of Bali's most famous attractions. Located at the edge of a stunning cliff, the temple offers breathtaking sunset views. Visitors can also enjoy the traditional Kecak dance performance during sunset. Uluwatu Temple is situated in the village of Pecatu, South Kuta, Badung Regency.

Dreamland Beach

Dreamland Beach is a beautiful white sand beach in Pecatu, Bali, known for its crystal-clear waters and strong waves. It's also popular among surfers. The beach is surrounded by towering coral cliffs, and you can get a stunning view of the coastline from the cliffs above.

Garuda Wisnu Kencana (GWK) Statue

The GWK Statue is a monumental cultural landmark in South Bali, representing Lord Vishnu riding the mythical bird, Garuda. Located on a 240-hectare cultural park, the GWK Statue is an important site for art, culture, and recreation in Bali.

Balangan Beach

Balangan Beach is a quieter alternative to the more crowded beaches like Kuta and Seminyak. With its fine white sand and clear blue waters, Balangan is a perfect place for relaxation and surfing. It's located near Dreamland Beach and offers a serene atmosphere.

Pandawa Beach

Pandawa Beach, once a hidden gem, is now one of the most visited beaches in Bali. Located in Kutuh Village, this beach is known for its clear waters and scenic cliffs. It has become popular among foreign tourists, especially those from Australia, who call it the "secret beach of Bali".

Nusa Dua Beach

Nusa Dua is a luxury resort area in South Bali, known for its pristine beaches, five-star hotels, and beautifully maintained gardens. The area offers a clean and tranquil environment, making it a perfect place to relax and enjoy upscale amenities.
